# Recruiting Prep

## What this workflow is

Prep for a recruiting cycle: research a firm, build and maintain the story bank, or prep for a specific conversation (coffee chat, networking call, interview). Use whenever the owner says "prep me for X" or "research firm Y."

## Prerequisites

- `context/me.md` and `context/voice.md` loaded
- `brain/INDEX.md` checked for existing notes on the firm or person
- Relevant files from the vault's `skills/` folder if the conversation is technical

## Inputs

| Field | Required | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Target | yes | Firm, person, or interview to prep for |
| Type | yes | Firm research / coffee chat / interview / application |
| Materials | no | Job posting, person's LinkedIn, past correspondence |
| Deadline | no | When the conversation or deadline is |

## Process

**Step 1: Gather.** Pull what's already in the brain about this target. For firm research, cover: what they do, strategy and recent work, size and structure, how they hire, and any school or personal connections. Facts only from real sources; anything unverified gets a placeholder.

**Step 2: Angle.** Connect the target to the owner's actual story from `context/me.md` and the story bank: real work experience, coursework, and genuine interests. Identify the two or three most relevant talking points, not a generic list.

**Step 3: Produce.** Depending on type:
- Firm research: one-page brief with a "why this firm" paragraph in the owner's voice
- Coffee chat / networking: five smart questions plus a 30-second intro tailored to the person
- Interview: likely questions with answer outlines drawn from the story bank, plus technical topics to review
- Application: talking points feeding into the actual materials

**Step 4: Save.** Write a dated brain note (`brain/notes/YYYY-MM-DD_recruit-<target>.md`) with what was produced and any new facts learned. Link to related notes. Update INDEX.md. After the real conversation happens, add what was said and any follow-ups to the same note.

## Story bank

Maintain `brain/references/story-bank.md`: a running list of concrete stories (situation, what the owner did, result) from work, school, and elsewhere. Every prep session that surfaces a new story adds it there.

## Known development areas

Ask the owner what they are working on in interviews and networking (being concise on the spot, running a cold coffee chat, telling stories tight, thinking on their feet). Record it here and have prep actively target it, not just note it.

## Quality gates

- [ ] Every firm fact is real and sourced; no invented deals, numbers, or names
- [ ] Talking points connect to the owner's actual experience, not generic ambition
- [ ] Anything written in their name passes `context/voice.md`
- [ ] No confidential work material leaks into external-facing content
- [ ] Brain note saved and indexed

## Edge cases

- Person or firm has almost no public info: say so plainly and prep general questions instead of padding with guesses.
- Conflicting info between sources: flag both with dates and let the owner decide.
