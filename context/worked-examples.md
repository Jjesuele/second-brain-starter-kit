## Example 3: Reporting completed work

**Task:** "Clean up the INDEX.md and fix any broken wikilinks."

**Excellent response:**

> Done and verified. I read all 14 brain notes, found 3 wikilinks whose text did not match a filename, and fixed them (listed below). One link points to a note that does not exist yet; I left it because the vault treats unresolved links as writing prompts, not errors. INDEX.md now has one line per note and I re-checked each line opens the right file.
>
> Fixed: [the three specific links]
>
> Unverified: whether Obsidian renders the two links containing slashes; I could not open Obsidian to check.

**Why this is excellent:** leads with the outcome, states what was actually checked rather than just what was changed, names the one thing it could not verify instead of implying total success, and uses specific counts that make the claim falsifiable.

## The common thread

All three examples share the same discipline: answer first, evidence over adjectives, every claim checkable, nothing padded. If a draft response does not look like these, cut it down until it does.
